🧅 Route this session through Tor?

This hides your IP, but it flips every open tab — including ones already signed in to Gmail, Facebook, etc. — onto Tor.

Your IP stays hidden from those sites, but keeping real-identity logins in the same window as anonymous browsing lets cookies, trackers and fingerprinting link your anonymous activity back to you. That — not an IP leak — is how mixing them de-anonymizes you.

For real anonymity, run tron --tor (a clean, wiped profile) and don’t sign in to personal accounts.
